]]> Once upon a time, there was a prince who went to a strange, unknown country. As he walked along, he came upon a princess sitting on a stool in a little wooden shed. 

“Why are you sitting there?” said the prince, but the princess did not answer. She just sat there looking like a statue. 

She looked so beautiful that the prince thought to himself she was just the perfect bride for him. In fact, he had set out on his mission to find himself just such a princess and this was the fourteenth country he had visited. He had been getting a little fed up with his quest and if he had not found this beautiful stranded maiden, he might have turned around and gone home that very minute. 

He shook her, but still she did not move. He sang her a little song (the birds flew away holding their ears) but she still did not move. 

“To be true!” exclaimed the prince. “She must be enchanted.” 

Just then, he heard the awful cackle of an old, wicked witch. 

“Get away!” she shouted at him in the most horrid, rasping voice he had ever heard. But the prince stood his ground for love of the maiden.

]]> Willie the Fly was the loneliest fly in the whole world. He didn’t have any friends, well, not really good ones, and he didn’t have any family.

Everybody else seemed to have a family and Willie thought, “Why don’t I have one?”

“Bye, bye,” they would call to their friends. “I’m going home.” and off to their home they would go, where someone would make their supper or put them to bed or clean their clothes. Home was a place where somebody told them what to do or even what not to do. Sometimes Willie had followed them to their homes and had seen it all.

Nobody told Willie off when he did something naughty; not that he wanted to be told off! But he would have liked somebody to have cared; nobody did.

He was left entirely on his own. Willie the fly just buzzed around the seashore, getting the occasional swipe if he came too close to people.

“Shoo! Go away!”

“Pesky flies.”

“Buzz off fly!”

Never a kind word or the offer of a jam tart; nothing!

The ants had families……

The bees had a proper home…..

“Why can’t I have a family?” he sighed. Then he went back to his little crack in the seaside wall with a tear just about to trickle off the end of his nose and settled down to sleep.

“I want a home,” he said to another fly the next day.

“Don’t be silly! Flies don’t have homes and families. We’re the wandering sort. We don’t settle down.”

“I do,” said Willie.

“Come on, fly, snap out of it! You’re a disgrace, a disgrace and an insult to flykind!”

“I’m not,” protested Willie.

“Well then, buzz off and prove it. Stop this silly dreaming.”

But Willie just couldn’t stop wishing, hoping, dreaming and longing for a family of his own – a place to come home to
